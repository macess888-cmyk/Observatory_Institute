# 3. OPERATING VALUES

## 3.1 Harmony

Harmony means preserving coordination without collapsing difference.

Operational meaning:

    domains remain distinguishable
    evidence types remain distinguishable
    supporting and adversarial findings coexist
    local and global observations remain separate
    observations and forecasts remain separate
    scientific and ethical controls remain coordinated

Boundary:

    Harmony
    ≠
    Forced Agreement

---

## 3.2 Compassion

Compassion means treating researchers, source owners, participants, and the public with dignity.

Operational meaning:

    unconventional hypotheses are not mocked
    source authors are represented fairly
    errors are corrected without humiliation
    uncertainty is communicated without fear amplification
    public interpretations avoid unnecessary alarm
    intellectual labour and authorship are respected

Boundary:

    Compassion
    ≠
    Lowered Evidence Standards

---

## 3.3 Integrity

Integrity means preserving what is actually known, unknown, supported, contradicted, missing, or unresolved.

Operational meaning:

    no silent correction
    no silent deletion
    no hidden transformation
    no selective evidence removal
    no forecast-to-observation collapse
    no retrospective rewriting
    no certainty inflation

Boundary:

    Confidence
    ≠
    Evidence

---

## 3.4 Love

Love is operationalized as protection from avoidable harm.

Operational meaning:

    protect the public from false warnings
    protect the public from false reassurance
    protect source owners from unauthorized redistribution
    protect collaborators from misattribution
    protect uncertain science from premature commercialization
    protect the research from pressure to produce dramatic conclusions

Boundary:

    Care
    ≠
    Claim Inflation

---

## 3.5 Humility

Humility means allowing unresolved questions to remain unresolved.

Operational meaning:

    UNKNOWN → HOLD
    failed reconstruction is valid
    null findings are preserved
    refusal is permitted
    conclusions remain bounded
    later evidence may revise current states

---

## 3.6 Stewardship

Stewardship means preserving evidence, context, permission, uncertainty, and future inspectability.

Operational meaning:

    source links preserved
    rights respected
    methods documented
    transformations receipted
    machine-readable outputs versioned
    contradictory evidence retained
    future reviewers can reconstruct the research path

# 5. REVERSE RECONSTRUCTION

Reverse reconstruction begins with a present claim, artifact, or conclusion and works backward.

Required chain:

    conclusion
    ←
    interpretation
    ←
    comparison
    ←
    reconstructed data
    ←
    displayed object
    ←
    processing
    ←
    instrument or model
    ←
    source condition

Reverse questions:

    What source supports this claim?
    What object was actually inspected?
    Was it an observation, forecast, model, or index?
    What transformation created the display?
    What information was lost?
    What assumptions were introduced?
    Can the source object be independently located?
    Can the conclusion be reproduced without hidden context?

Reverse failure condition:

    claim exists
    but
    source or derivation cannot be reconstructed

Disposition:

    HOLD

---

# 7. BIDIRECTIONAL ENGINEERING

Every important mapping must be testable in both directions.

## 7.1 Instrument-to-display direction

    instrument specification
    →
    expected display characteristics

Examples:

    missing registration
    →
    black vertical band

    local impulsive lightning
    →
    high-amplitude vertical feature

    narrow-band technogenic source
    →
    horizontal spectral line

## 7.2 Display-to-instrument direction

    observed display feature
    →
    compatible instrument or source conditions

Examples:

    black vertical band
    →
    missing-data candidate

    white vertical band
    →
    local lightning, impulsive interference,
    clipping, or saturation candidate

    horizontal line
    →
    technogenic narrow-band source candidate

A display feature may map to multiple causes.

It must not be forced into one explanation without discriminating evidence.
