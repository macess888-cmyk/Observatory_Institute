# 11. COMPARATIVE REVIEW QUESTIONS

    RQ-001
    Are all displayed objects authentic and attributable?

    RQ-002
    Are observation times, retrieval times, issue times, and forecast windows distinguishable?

    RQ-003
    Are objects correctly classified as observation, model, forecast, or derived index?

    RQ-004
    Are saturation, contamination, missing data, and instrument-health states visible?

    RQ-005
    Can every visualization be reconstructed from its upstream source?

    RQ-006
    Are any apparent cross-domain relationships statistically distinguishable from coincidence?

    RQ-007
    Do candidate relationships survive time shifting, surrogate data, negative controls, event removal, declustering, and independent replication?

    RQ-008
    Is there a physically plausible and quantitatively sufficient causal pathway?

    RQ-009
    Can any claim be made prospectively before the event rather than retrospectively afterward?

    RQ-010
    Does the interface induce users to infer relationships merely because panels are displayed together?

    RQ-011
    Is scientific-source authority being transferred to surrounding interpretive or commercial claims?

    RQ-012
    Is any warning, prediction, escalation, or action justified?

# 12. ACCEPTANCE BOUNDARY

A source may be admitted into the comparative corpus when:

    publisher identity is established
    document or product identity is established
    source URL is preserved
    retrieval time is recorded
    object class is declared
    claim relevance is stated
    limitations are visible
    supporting or adversarial posture is declared
    local copy or archive reference exists
    integrity metadata can be preserved

Admission into the source corpus does not mean the source claim is accepted.

---

# 13. HOLD CONDITIONS

A source remains HOLD when:

    source identity is uncertain
    timestamp is unresolved
    future date is unexplained
    object type is ambiguous
    model is presented as observation
    derived index is presented as direct measurement
    transformation history is absent
    saturation is unresolved
    licence is unknown
    archive is unavailable
    claim cannot be separated from interpretation
    metadata is incomplete
