This document registers the strongest publicly available hypotheses, studies, conceptual models, and reported observations that could support relationships among:

    solar activity
    solar wind
    magnetospheric conditions
    ionospheric conditions
    atmospheric electricity
    Schumann-resonance observations
    lithospheric processes
    electromagnetic earthquake precursors
    seismic activity
    planetary or tidal forcing

The purpose is to represent each supporting position fairly before adversarial testing.

Registration does not mean endorsement.

A reported correlation does not establish:

    mechanism
    causation
    prediction
    warning authority
    global applicability
    independent replication

Every hypothesis must remain distinguishable from the observations used to motivate it.

# 5. HYPOTHESIS SH-001 — SOLAR-WIND PROTON DENSITY AND LARGE EARTHQUAKES

    Hypothesis ID:
    SH-001

    Source:
    Marchitelli, Harabaglia, Troise, De Natale, and collaborators

    Publication:
    Scientific Reports

    Title:
    On the correlation between solar activity and large earthquakes worldwide

    Publication year:
    2020

    DOI:
    10.1038/s41598-020-67860-3

    Source URL:
    https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-020-67860-3

    Source type:
    PEER-REVIEWED OPEN-ACCESS PAPER

    Status:
    STATISTICAL ASSOCIATION REPORTED
    MECHANISM PROPOSED
    CONTESTED
    REPLICATION REQUIRED

## 5.1 Reported claim

The authors report a relationship between measured solar-wind proton density and the occurrence of large earthquakes in a worldwide catalogue.

The reported association is strongest for earthquakes occurring during a one-day interval after proton density decreases below a selected threshold following elevated density.

## 5.2 Data used

    Solar-wind source:
    SOHO measurements near the Sun–Earth L1 region

    Solar variables examined:
    proton density
    proton velocity
    proton flux
    dynamic pressure

    Earthquake catalogue:
    ISC-GEM

    Catalogue interval:
    approximately 1996 through 2016

    Main completeness threshold:
    M ≥ 5.6

    Response variable:
    daily earthquake occurrence or count

## 5.3 Reported statistical result

The paper reports that:

    proton density produced the strongest association
    proton flux also showed a weaker association
    the selected lag was approximately one day
    reported significance was high under the study procedure
    the association reportedly increased with higher magnitude thresholds

The paper also used a testing subdivision and a Molchan-style forecasting assessment.

## 5.4 Proposed mechanism

The authors propose a tentative pathway involving:

    solar-wind proton density
    →
    altered ionosphere–Earth electrical potential
    →
    electrical discharge or induced potential
    →
    conductive fault structures
    →
    reverse piezoelectric stress or strain pulse
    →
    triggering of an already critically stressed fault

The authors do not claim that solar forcing creates tectonic stress.

The proposed role is a small final trigger acting on a fault already near failure.

## 5.5 Supporting value

This paper is presently one of the clearest peer-reviewed quantitative claims supporting a solar-wind–earthquake relationship because it:

    defines specific solar-wind variables
    uses a global earthquake catalogue
    identifies a temporal lag
    reports statistical tests
    proposes a physical mechanism
    includes a test-period analysis

## 5.6 Required adversarial tests

The claim must be tested against:

    catalogue-version dependence
    catalogue completeness
    magnitude-threshold selection
    proton-density threshold selection
    lag selection
    daily binning
    missing SOHO data
    autocorrelation
    earthquake clustering
    aftershock sequences
    non-Poisson event occurrence
    multiple comparisons
    parameter search burden
    sensitivity to single major events
    alternative solar-wind spacecraft
    alternative seismic catalogues
    rolling rather than calendar-day windows
    out-of-sample periods after 2016
    prospective timestamped forecasts
    independent code reproduction

## 5.7 Falsification conditions

The hypothesis would be materially weakened if:

    the result disappears under preregistered thresholds
    the result disappears after declustering
    the result fails in later untouched data
    similar associations appear under randomized time shifts
    the reported lag is not stable
    the relationship depends on one catalogue version
    an alternative solar-wind dataset does not reproduce it
    the proposed electrical forcing is quantitatively insufficient

    CORPUS ADMISSION:
    YES

    CLAIM ACCEPTANCE:
    NO

    CURRENT STATUS:
    CONTESTED / REPLICATION REQUIRED

    WARNING OR PREDICTION:
    NOT AUTHORIZED

# 6. HYPOTHESIS SH-002 — LITHOSPHERE–ATMOSPHERE–IONOSPHERE–MAGNETOSPHERE COUPLING

    Hypothesis ID:
    SH-002

    Source family:
    Pulinets, Ouzounov, Karelin, Davidenko, and related researchers

    Representative publication:
    Lithosphere–Atmosphere–Ionosphere–Magnetosphere Coupling—
    A Concept for Pre-Earthquake Signals Generation

    Publication:
    Geophysical Monograph Series

    Publication year:
    2018

    DOI:
    10.1002/9781119156949.ch6

    Source URL:
    https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/9781119156949.ch6

    Source type:
    CONCEPTUAL SCIENTIFIC MODEL / REVIEW CHAPTER

    Status:
    CONCEPTUAL MODEL
    OBSERVATIONS REPORTED
    CONTESTED
    QUANTITATIVE MODEL COMPLETENESS REQUIRED

## 6.1 Core claim

The LAIMC or LAIC family proposes that earthquake-preparation processes may generate measurable anomalies across the lithosphere, lower atmosphere, upper atmosphere, ionosphere, and near-Earth environment.

Candidate observations may include:

    radon or gas release
    air ionization
    atmospheric conductivity change
    aerosol charging
    electric-field variation
    thermal anomalies
    ionospheric total-electron-content variation
    plasma-density variation
    electromagnetic variation

## 6.2 Candidate forward pathway

    accumulating tectonic stress
    →
    lithospheric electrical, chemical, thermal, or gas-release process
    →
    atmospheric boundary-layer change
    →
    alteration of atmospheric electrical conductivity
    →
    ionospheric electrodynamic response
    →
    observable satellite or ground anomaly

## 6.3 Supporting value

The hypothesis provides an explicit proposed bridge between otherwise separate domains.

It also encourages:

    multi-parameter observation
    ground and satellite comparison
    spatial localization
    temporal sequencing
    physical-process modelling

## 6.4 Current limitation

The representative chapter describes the approach as conceptual rather than one fully integrated quantitative model spanning all domains.

Observed anomalies may also arise from:

    ordinary space weather
    tropospheric weather
    geomagnetic disturbance
    instrument effects
    seasonal ionospheric variability
    human activity
    post-selection of unusual intervals

## 6.5 Required evidence burden

    event-independent anomaly definition
    source-localized physical mechanism
    quantitative energy or charge transfer
    atmospheric transport model
    ionospheric response model
    background climatology
    solar and geomagnetic controls
    weather controls
    geographically matched controls
    non-earthquake controls
    blinded event windows
    prospective replication

Schumann resonances arise primarily from electromagnetic excitation of the Earth–ionosphere cavity, especially by global lightning activity.

This establishes a physical pathway:

    lightning
    →
    Earth–ionosphere cavity excitation
    →
    ELF resonance structure
    →
    station measurement

Observed changes in amplitude, frequency, bandwidth, or spectral appearance may reflect changes in:

    global lightning distribution

    day–night structure
    seasonal conditions
    solar or geomagnetic forcing
    local atmospheric electricity

    instrument gain
    saturation

    instrument calibration
    local weather controls
    lightning-network controls
    gain and clipping metadata
    missing-data flags

    geographically coherent change

# 12. HYPOTHESIS SH-008 — LUNI-SOLAR TIDAL EARTHQUAKE TRIGGERING

    Hypothesis ID:
    SH-008

    Status:
    PHYSICALLY PLAUSIBLE SMALL STRESS PERTURBATION
    EVENT-LEVEL PREDICTIVE VALUE UNRESOLVED

## 12.1 Core claim

Gravitational tides produced by the Moon and Sun alter stress in Earth’s crust.

A sufficiently small tidal stress change might influence rupture timing on a fault already close to failure.

## 12.2 Supporting foundation

Earth tides are physically real and calculable.

The unresolved question is not whether tidal stresses exist.

The unresolved question is whether they provide:

    statistically reliable triggering
    stable event-selection value
    useful prediction
    magnitude information
    location information

## 12.3 Required distinctions

    tidal stress exists
    ≠
    earthquake triggering established

    statistical modulation
    ≠
    deterministic prediction

    selected event alignment
    ≠
    prospective skill

    full earthquake catalogue
    declustering
    focal-mechanism-specific stress projection
    ocean-loading correction
    location-specific tide calculation
    magnitude-threshold preregistration
    multiple-testing correction
    prospective replication
