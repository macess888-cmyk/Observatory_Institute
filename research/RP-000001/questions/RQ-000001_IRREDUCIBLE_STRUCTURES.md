# Research Question

What are the irreducible structures required for organized understanding?

---

# Motivation

Understanding appears to depend on multiple interacting structures, including observations, evidence, relationships, memory, language, time, and correction.

It is not yet established which of these structures are necessary, which are derivative, or whether other irreducible structures remain unidentified.

---

# Scope

This question investigates candidate structures such as:

- Entity
- Relationship
- Observation
- Evidence
- Pattern
- Process
- Memory
- Language
- Time
- Context
- Correction
- Stewardship

The list is provisional and does not imply that every candidate is irreducible.

---

# Non-Assumptions

This research question does not assume that:

- relationships are primary,
- observations are sufficient,
- knowledge and understanding are equivalent,
- institutional preservation guarantees continuity,
- current ontology is complete.

---

# Evidence Required

Evidence may include:

- cross-domain comparisons,
- counterexamples,
- failure cases,
- reconstruction tests,
- dependency analysis,
- historical examples,
- operational observations from the Institute,
- formal models.
