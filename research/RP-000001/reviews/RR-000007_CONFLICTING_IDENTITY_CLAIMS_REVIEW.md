## 1. Review Purpose

This review evaluates whether ROB-000007 correctly represents conflict between authentic immutable identity claims.

It examines:

* whether the claims are truly incompatible;
* whether scope, time, or semantics can reconcile them;
* what evidentiary precedence is legitimate;
* whether authority may determine operational precedence;
* what counts as independent corroboration;
* how unresolved conflict should propagate into decisions, models, and publications;
* whether conflict identity is distinct from claim identity;
* when irreversible action may still be justified.

## 3. Summary of the Observation

ROB-000007 presents two authentic immutable records:

```text
R1:
TARGET-1 = Member A at t1
```

```text
R2:
TARGET-1 = Member B at t1
```

The records share the same stated time and scope, while Member A and Member B are distinct.

The observation concludes that the institution can know that a conflict exists without knowing which target binding is correct.

Its central candidate finding is:

> Authentic immutable records can preserve incompatible target-binding claims. Organized understanding remains possible at the record, conflict, and evidentiary levels even when target identity remains unresolved.

---

## 4. Are the Claims Truly Incompatible?

The claims are incompatible only if all relevant dimensions match.

The following must be equivalent:

* reference;
* namespace;
* target type;
* binding type;
* effective time;
* effective scope;
* continuity criterion;
* uniqueness rule.

If any dimension differs, the conflict may be apparent rather than real.

For example:

```text
R1:
TARGET-1 = Member A as physical occupant
```

```text
R2:
TARGET-1 = Member B as current role-holder
```

These may both be valid.

### Determination

**INCOMPATIBILITY: CONDITIONALLY ESTABLISHED**

A conflict must be normalized across scope, time, semantics, and target type before being treated as irreducible contradiction.

## 6. Scope and Namespace Review

The same reference may be valid in different namespaces.

```text
System X / TARGET-1
```

may differ from:

```text
System Y / TARGET-1
```

If namespace is absent, a local identity may be mistaken for a global identity.

Therefore:

```text
Reference Equality
        ≠
Identity Equality
```

without scope equality.

### Determination

**EXPLICIT NAMESPACE: REQUIRED**

---

## 7. Temporal Precision Review

Two records may appear simultaneous while covering different effective intervals.

A coarse timestamp may conceal valid sequential rebinding.

For example:

```text
R1 valid until 10:00:00.500
R2 valid from 10:00:00.501
```

Both may be reported as `10:00`.

### Determination

**TEMPORAL PRECISION MUST MATCH THE CLAIM STRENGTH**

---

## 8. Binding-Semantics Review

The phrase:

```text
TARGET-1 = Member A
```

is incomplete unless the relationship is defined.

Possible meanings include:

* occupied by;
* owned by;
* controlled by;
* assigned to;
* represented by;
* located at;
* historically associated with;
* currently acting as.

Different semantics may produce valid but non-equivalent claims.

### Determination

**BINDING SEMANTICS: REQUIRED**

---

## 9. Conflict Identity Versus Claim Identity

Each record has claim identity.

The conflict is a separate relationship among claims.

```text
Claim R1
        ↘
         Conflict IC-001
        ↗
Claim R2
```

The conflict has its own:

* identifier;
* type;
* status;
* scope;
* evidence requirements;
* resolution history.

### Determination

**CONFLICT IDENTITY: DISTINCT AND SUPPORTED**

---

## 10. What Authenticity Establishes

Authenticity may establish:

* source association;
* issuance;
* record integrity;
* historical existence.

It does not establish target correctness.

Two authentic records may conflict honestly.

### Determination

**AUTHENTICITY IS COMPATIBLE WITH CONTRADICTION**

---

## 11. Authority as Operational Precedence

An institution may need a controlling record for immediate operation.

A designated authority may choose R1 as controlling.

This can establish:

```text
Operational State: use R1
```

It cannot establish:

```text
Epistemic State: R1 is proven true
```

### Determination

**AUTHORITY MAY DETERMINE OPERATIONAL PRECEDENCE**

**AUTHORITY ALONE MAY NOT DETERMINE EPISTEMIC TRUTH**

---

## 12. When Authority Matters Evidentially

Authority may be relevant when it correlates with:

* better access;
* defined responsibility;
* validated procedure;
* stronger instrumentation;
* legal assignment;
* controlled chain of custody.

Its weight derives from the supporting process, not status alone.

### Determination

**AUTHORITY MAY CONTRIBUTE EVIDENTIARY WEIGHT WHEN PROCESS-GROUNDED**

---

## 13. Expertise Review

Expertise may increase credibility.

However, expertise does not guarantee:

* correct observation;
* correct target;
* absence of bias;
* complete visibility;
* proper timing.

### Determination

**EXPERTISE: RELEVANT BUT NON-DECISIVE**

---

## 14. Recency Review

A later record may reflect:

* correction;
* rebinding;
* updated evidence;
* replacement;
* a new error.

Recency alone is insufficient.

A later claim gains precedence only when its relation to the earlier claim is explicit and supported.

### Determination

**RECENCY ALONE: INSUFFICIENT**

---

## 15. Explicit Supersession

A supersession claim should include:

* superseded record;
* authority;
* reason;
* supporting evidence;
* effective time;
* scope;
* downstream effect.

A bare statement of supersession is procedurally informative but epistemically incomplete.

### Determination

**SUPERSESSION REQUIRES JUSTIFICATION**

---

## 16. Evidence Precedence

Legitimate precedence may consider:

* directness;
* independence;
* resolution;
* temporal precision;
* target specificity;
* chain of custody;
* reproducibility;
* corroboration;
* source reliability;
* method validity.

No universal single-factor ordering is supported.

### Determination

**EVIDENCE PRECEDENCE: MULTI-FACTOR AND CONTEXT-DEPENDENT**

---

## 17. Independent Corroboration

Multiple records strengthen a claim only when their evidentiary lineages are meaningfully independent.

Three records derived from one source remain one lineage.

A valid review should distinguish:

```text
Record Count
```

from:

```text
Independent Evidence Count
```

### Determination

**EVIDENCE LINEAGE: REQUIRED**

---

## 18. Shared-Origin Error

Agreement across dependent records can amplify confidence without adding evidence.

This produces inherited corroboration.

### Determination

**SHARED-ORIGIN CORROBORATION MUST NOT BE COUNTED AS INDEPENDENT SUPPORT**

---

## 19. Majority Review

A majority may guide operations when no better rule exists.

It cannot automatically establish truth.

Majority strength depends on:

* independence;
* method quality;
* common-source risk;
* target visibility;
* incentive alignment.

### Determination

**MAJORITY: OPERATIONALLY RELEVANT, EPISTEMICALLY LIMITED**

---

## 20. Evidence Weight Versus Resolution

One claim may have stronger evidence without fully resolving the conflict.

The institution may record:

```text
Preferred Claim: R1
Confidence: MODERATE
Conflict Status: UNRESOLVED
```

This is more accurate than declaring complete resolution.

### Determination

**PROVISIONAL PREFERENCE: VALID**

---

## 21. Unknown Preservation

Where conflict remains unresolved, the institution should preserve:

* candidate bindings;
* source records;
* lineage;
* confidence;
* limitations;
* action restrictions;
* required evidence.

This converts contradiction into structured unknown knowledge.

### Determination

**UNKNOWN PRESERVATION: REQUIRED**

---

## 22. Decision Under Uncertainty

A decision may be necessary before identity resolution.

Valid decisions may include:

* containment;
* temporary dual treatment;
* reversible suspension;
* evidence request;
* restricted access;
* deferred irreversible action.

The decision should identify its uncertainty basis.

### Determination

**DECISION WITHOUT CONCLUSION: VALID**

---

## 23. Irreversible Action

Irreversible action under unresolved identity conflict may still be justified in exceptional cases where:

* delay creates greater harm;
* the action applies safely to all candidate targets;
* evidence strongly favours one claim;
* no reversible option exists;
* authority and consequence are explicit;
* uncertainty is documented.

The evidentiary threshold should rise with consequence.

### Determination

**IRREVERSIBLE ACTION: NOT ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED**

**REQUIRES HEIGHTENED JUSTIFICATION**

---

## 24. Consequence Scaling

The model supports:

```text
Higher Consequence
        │
        ▼
Higher Identity and Evidence Threshold
```

Low-consequence reversible actions may proceed with weaker identity.

High-consequence irreversible actions require stronger continuity and reconciliation.

### Determination

**SUPPORTED**

## 28. Propagation Into Models

A model using R1 must record:

* dependency on R1;
* conflict with R2;
* evidence strength;
* confidence;
* revision conditions.

Otherwise, the conflict disappears from downstream knowledge.

### Determination

**UNCERTAINTY PROPAGATION: REQUIRED**

## 29. Branching Models

Where conflict is material, a model may preserve alternative branches:

```text

    Consequence A

If R2 is correct:
    Consequence B
```

This supports analysis without premature collapse.

### Determination

**BRANCHING REPRESENTATION: SUPPORTED**

## 30. Confidence-Weighted Models

A model may assign provisional confidence to competing branches.

However, numerical confidence should not be invented without a defensible method.

### Determination

**CONFIDENCE WEIGHTING: CONDITIONALLY VALID**

## 31. Publication Boundary

A publication should not present a resolved identity where the underlying records remain contested.

It should disclose:

* conflict;
* evidence basis;
* selected interpretation;
* limitations;
* alternative claim;
* status.

### Determination

**CONFLICT DISCLOSURE: REQUIRED**
