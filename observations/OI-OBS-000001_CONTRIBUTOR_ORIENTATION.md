## Operational Question

Can a new contributor understand the Observatory Institute from the repository alone?

---

## Observation Context

The Observatory Institute completed its initial constitutional, research, semantic, architectural, and contributor foundations.

The repository currently contains:

- Founding Prospectus
- Constitution
- Research Program
- Foundational Ontology
- Institutional Grammar
- Institutional Architecture Map
- Architecture Index
- START HERE guide
- Institutional Decision Record
- Typed registries

This observation evaluates whether those artifacts collectively provide a coherent entry path for a contributor without requiring direct explanation from the founding stewards.

---

## Observed Entry Path

A new contributor can currently follow this sequence:

1. README
2. START HERE
3. Architecture Index
4. Founding Prospectus
5. Constitution
6. Research Program
7. Foundational Ontology
8. Institutional Grammar
9. Institutional Architecture Map
10. Institutional Decision Records

---

## Positive Signals

- The Institute has a clear stated purpose.
- Foundational artifacts have typed identifiers.
- A recommended reading order exists.
- Institutional structure is represented visually.
- Architectural reasoning is preserved.
- Research, ontology, grammar, and architecture are distinguished.
- Git history preserves institutional evolution.

---

## Friction Observed

- Several root-level placeholder files remain incomplete.
- Some platform files remain empty.
- The README may not yet link directly to the START HERE guide.
- The Architecture Index may not yet contain clickable document links.
- No contributor has yet completed the path independently.
- Operational submission and review procedures do not yet exist.

---

## Current Assessment

The repository appears capable of orienting a motivated contributor at a conceptual level.

Independent contributor validation has not yet occurred.

Therefore, full onboarding capability is not yet proven.

---

## Status Determination

PARTIAL

---

## Evidence Boundary

Repository structure and document availability provide evidence of intended contributor orientation.

They do not prove that an unfamiliar contributor can successfully navigate, understand, and contribute without assistance.

---

## Next Required Evidence

A person without prior involvement should attempt the documented reading path and record:

- what they understood,
- what remained unclear,
- what they could locate,
- where they required assistance,
- whether they could identify an appropriate first contribution.

---

## Operational Conclusion

Contributor orientation is implemented but not independently validated.

UNKNOWN remains where external contributor evidence is absent.
