# Purpose

This standard defines the common lifecycle followed by institutional artifacts within the Observatory Institute.

The objective is to ensure that every artifact evolves through a transparent, inspectable, and reproducible process.

---

# Scope

Unless explicitly exempted, this lifecycle applies to:

- Research artifacts
- Operational artifacts
- Governance artifacts
- Architectural artifacts
- Publications
- Standards
- Maps
- Registries

---

# Lifecycle

## Stage 1

Idea

A potential contribution has been identified.

No institutional commitment exists.

---

## Stage 2

Observation

Evidence, questions, or motivation are recorded.

---

## Stage 3

Proposal

A candidate artifact is described.

Purpose and expected value are documented.

---

## Stage 4

Draft

The artifact is created.

It remains open to revision.

---

## Stage 5

Review

The artifact is inspected for:

- clarity
- consistency
- completeness
- architectural fit

---

## Stage 6

Accepted

The artifact becomes part of the Institute.

---

## Stage 7

Active

The artifact is used operationally.

Operational experience becomes evidence.

---

## Stage 8

Revised

Improvements are incorporated.

History is preserved.

---

## Stage 9

Superseded

A newer artifact replaces the current one.

The previous artifact remains preserved.

---

## Stage 10

Archived

The artifact is retained as part of institutional history.

---

# Lifecycle Principles

No artifact is deleted solely because it becomes obsolete.

History is preserved.

Evidence accompanies revision.

Reasoning accompanies decisions.

Operational experience guides refinement.

---

# Foundational Principle

Institutional learning is cumulative.

Every revision should strengthen future understanding without erasing historical context.
