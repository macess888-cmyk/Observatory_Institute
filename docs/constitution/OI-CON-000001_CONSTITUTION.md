# PREAMBLE

The Observatory Institute is established to advance humanity's capacity to observe, understand, preserve, and responsibly improve complex systems.

We recognize that knowledge is never complete.

Understanding is continually refined through observation, evidence, reason, and responsible stewardship.

This Constitution establishes the enduring principles that guide the Institute, independent of any particular technology, discipline, or generation.

---

# ARTICLE I

## Purpose

The Observatory Institute exists to strengthen humanity's ability to understand reality through careful observation, trustworthy evidence, reproducible inquiry, and responsible stewardship.

---

# ARTICLE II

## Identity

The Institute is an independent research institution.

Its purpose is not to preserve certainty.

Its purpose is to preserve the ability to continually improve understanding.

---

# ARTICLE III

## Principles

The Institute shall seek to:

Observe honestly.

Preserve evidence faithfully.

Reason carefully.

Revise responsibly.

Teach openly.

Steward knowledge across generations.

---

# ARTICLE IV

## Stewardship

Every generation inherits unfinished work.

Every generation is responsible for improving that work where evidence justifies change.

---

# ARTICLE V

## Public Benefit

The Institute exists for the benefit of present and future generations.

Knowledge developed by the Institute should strengthen humanity's collective capacity for understanding.

---

# ARTICLE VI

## Future Generations

The Institute recognizes future researchers, educators, engineers, students, and citizens as beneficiaries of its work.

Its records should therefore remain understandable, inspectable, and reproducible whenever practical.

---

# ARTICLE VII

## Amendment

This Constitution may evolve.

Every amendment shall preserve the historical record and include a documented rationale.

---

# Constitutional Motto

Leave the Institute brighter than you found it.
