# Purpose

The Institutional Grammar defines how the Observatory Institute organizes knowledge.

It establishes the semantic relationships between institutional concepts.

The grammar supports consistency across research, governance, software, education, publications, and future institutional growth.

# Definitions

## Institution

An enduring organization responsible for preserving and advancing knowledge across generations.

---

## Program

A coordinated body of long-term work pursuing a shared purpose.

---

## Theme

A persistent area of investigation within a program.

---

## Platform

A reusable system that enables research or institutional capability.

---

## Capability

A repeatable function performed by the Institute.

---

## Artifact

A preserved output produced by institutional activity.

Examples include:

- Publications
- Standards
- Software
- Reports
- Maps
- Ontologies

---

## Observation

A recorded insight, measurement, question, or discovery that contributes to understanding.

Observations are the smallest persistent units of institutional learning.

---

# Relationship Rules

Programs contain Themes.

Themes organize Platforms.

Platforms enable Capabilities.

Capabilities produce Artifacts.

Artifacts preserve Observations.

Observations inform future Programs.

---

# Architectural Principle

Every institutional object should occupy one primary place within the grammar.

Relationships between objects should be explicit rather than implied.

---

# Guiding Principle

The grammar exists to reduce ambiguity, improve communication, and preserve institutional clarity.
