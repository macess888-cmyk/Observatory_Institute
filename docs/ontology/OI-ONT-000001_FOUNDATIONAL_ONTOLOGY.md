## Purpose

The Foundational Ontology defines the fundamental concepts from which all Observatory Institute research, software, publications, and institutional artifacts derive their meaning.

It is intended to provide a shared semantic foundation across disciplines.

---

# First Principle

Reality exists independently of our descriptions of it.

Observation improves understanding but does not define reality.

---

# Fundamental Objects

The Observatory Institute presently recognizes the following foundational object categories.

## Entity

Something that exists.

---

## Relationship

A meaningful connection between entities.

---

## Observation

A recorded interaction between an observer and reality.

---

## Evidence

Information capable of supporting or challenging understanding.

---

## Pattern

A reproducible regularity observed across one or more observations.

---

## Process

An ordered sequence of change.

---

## System

A collection of entities and relationships exhibiting coherent behavior.

---

## Change

A measurable difference across time.

---

## Steward

An entity that accepts responsibility for preserving or improving something.

---

## Institution

A steward capable of preserving knowledge across generations.

---

## Future

The space of possible states not yet observed.

---

# Foundational Hypothesis

Relationships are fundamental to understanding complex systems.

This hypothesis remains subject to continual observation, challenge, and refinement.

---

# Motto

Observe.

Understand.

Steward.

Improve.
